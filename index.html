About the Project
WHAT IS THIS PROJECT?
📊
The Goal
This project simulates the future trajectory of Pakistan's policy interest rate as set by the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP). Rather than making a single prediction, it generates hundreds of plausible future scenarios, giving a probabilistic view of where rates could go.
🏦
Why It Matters
Interest rates drive borrowing costs, inflation, currency value, and investment decisions. By simulating the rate's future distribution, banks, investors, and policymakers can quantify interest rate risk and make better-informed decisions.
🎯
The Data
The model is calibrated using historical SBP policy rate data. From this data, we estimate three key parameters: the long-run average rate the market reverts toward, the speed of that reversion, and the random volatility in rate changes. These parameters then drive the simulation forward in time.
The Mathematics
THE VASICEK MODEL
The Vasicek model (1977) is one of the foundational models in quantitative finance for describing how interest rates evolve over time. Unlike a random walk, it assumes rates are pulled back toward a long-run equilibrium — a property called mean reversion. This reflects the real-world observation that rates don't drift to infinity; central banks anchor them around target levels.
Stochastic Differential Equation
dr(t) = a · ( b − r(t) ) · dt + σ · dW(t)
r(t) = interest rate at time t  ·  a = speed of mean reversion  ·  b = long-run mean rate
σ = volatility of rate changes  ·  dW(t) = Wiener process (random shock)
The Process
HOW IT WORKS
01
Load Historical Data
The app reads the SBP policy rate CSV file containing historical rate decisions. This gives us a real time series to work with — the actual rate Pakistan's central bank has set over the years.
02
Estimate Parameters
From the historical data, three parameters are calculated: the long-run mean (b) as the historical average rate, volatility (σ) as the standard deviation of rate changes, and the mean reversion speed (a) which you can adjust in the simulator.
03
Simulate Paths
Using the discretised Vasicek equation, the model simulates 500 independent future rate paths by stepping forward in monthly intervals. At each step, a random shock drawn from a normal distribution is added, creating the characteristic stochastic spread you see in the charts.
04
Visualise & Analyse
All 500 paths are aggregated to produce the mean forecast, 68% and 95% confidence bands, and a 3D probability density surface showing exactly where rates are most likely to land at each point in time. The terminal distribution histogram shows the full spread of possible rates at the end of your chosen horizon.
Honest Assessment
LIMITATIONS & FUTURE WORK
⚠️No External Factors
The model only uses the rate's own history. Real-world SBP decisions are driven by inflation, GDP, unemployment, PKR/USD exchange rate, and global rates — none of which are captured here.
📉Negative Rates Possible
Vasicek's math can produce negative interest rates — impossible in Pakistan's monetary reality. This is a known structural flaw of the single-factor model.
🔒Fixed Parameters
Mean reversion speed, long-run mean, and volatility are held constant. Pakistan's macro environment has shifted dramatically across regimes, so these likely change over time.
📋Short Data Window
Calibrated on a limited historical sample. A longer or different window could yield meaningfully different parameters and simulation outcomes.
Extension 01
VAR Model
Include CPI, GDP, unemployment, and exchange rate in a Vector Autoregression framework to capture macro interdependencies.
Extension 02
CIR Model
The Cox-Ingersoll-Ross model extends Vasicek while constraining rates to stay positive — more realistic for Pakistan's context.
Extension 03
Regime Switching
A two-state Markov model to reflect Pakistan's distinct tightening and easing cycles with time-varying parameters.
